# CONCEPT — Dermamedics Makati

*First Blacklyne Philippines build. Senior Creative Director concept document. Written before a single line of code. Self-verified against the Blacklyne DNA Check at the foot of this file.*

---

## ARCHETYPE

**Name: "The Transparent Clinic" — a doctor-led, Korean-influenced aesthetic medicine practice that publishes what others hide.**

Every competitor in Metro Manila — Belo, Aivee, Luminisce — hides price behind a "book a consultation" wall (verified: none publish a clean public price list). Dermamedics' patients already praise it for the opposite. So the archetype is not "luxury beauty clinic" (everyone claims that) — it is the clinic that treats the patient as an intelligent adult: shows the methodology, names the doctors, frames the cost honestly, and lets a free aesthetic-doctor consultation do the closing.

**Why this archetype fits Dermamedics specifically:**
- **15 years** ("Elevating Beauty since 2010") — heritage in a market full of two-year-old Instagram clinics. That is a credential, not a tagline, so it leads the stat band and the about story.
- **Doctor-led** — three named doctors (Dr. Leslie Suyen Tan, Dr. Adelsa Tee, and "Doc Jam", the celebrated 9F aesthetic doctor). Faces and accountability, not an anonymous chain.
- **Transparent pricing** — the patient-praised differentiator. We make it the spine of the site, not a footnote.
- **Centuria Medical Makati** — the premium medical building in Century City is borrowed credibility. We surface the building as a trust proof, not just an address.
- **Korean clinical methodology** — Korean Mesolipo, Korean fillers (Neuramis/Yvoire), Korean PDO threads, Ultraformer HIFU. Genuine clinical lineage, not marketing fluff — so it earns its own educational page.

## COLOUR RATIONALE

Locked palette, justified shade by shade.

- **Deep aubergine `#3D2737` (brand main)** — Asian-luxury register without K-pop trend or Belo-clinical cold. Aubergine is a desaturated wine/plum: it reads as *medical-grade premium* (close to clinical navy in weight) but carries warmth and a cosmetic, skin-adjacent undertone that pure navy or black never could. It is unmistakably *not* the all-pink palette every Manila aesthetic salon defaults to, and *not* the sterile black-and-white of the corporate giant.
- **Pearl cream `#F4EFE8` (surface)** — warm off-white (never pure `#FFF`). Signals luxury spa warmth while staying clean enough for medical credibility. It softens the aubergine so the site feels *cared-for*, not *operated-on*.
- **Rose champagne `#C9A284` (accent)** — the single warm metallic. Used with restraint: hairline rules, eyebrow ticks, the wordmark mark, hover states, the "D". It carries the Korean-glow / skin-tone warmth without tipping into rose-gold cliché. Treated like brass in the trade builds — an accent, never a flood.

**Why NOT pink / rose-gold:** it is the undifferentiated default of ~90% of PH aesthetic clinics — reads feminine-salon, cheap, and forgettable. Aubergine instantly separates Dermamedics from the SkinStation/salon tier.
**Why NOT black-and-white clinical:** that is the Belo-corporate register — intimidating, cold, and exactly the "judged" feeling the Filipino aesthetic patient fears (see patient psyche). Cream + aubergine is authoritative *and* warm.

## TYPOGRAPHY RATIONALE

- **Display: Bodoni Moda (variable, 500–800)** — high-contrast didone editorial serif. *Note on the locked spec:* the brief locks **PP Right Serif Display**, a commercial Pangram Pangram face we cannot license/obtain for this build (same situation handled on the Angel Plumbing build, where Caslon→licensed Migra). Bodoni Moda is the chosen freely-licensable substitute that serves the **identical intent** — an editorial, fashion-grade serif for a premium beauty register — and is distinct from every prior Blacklyne build. Its high stroke contrast is the language of beauty editorial (Vogue, Pat McGrath, the aiveeclinic/luminisce tier), which is precisely the aesthetic-medicine luxury cue we want. Set on warm cream with generous spacing it reads luxe, not cold.
- **Body: Inter (variable, 450–600)** — neutral, highly legible across a multi-generational 28–55 patient audience, on small Filipino mobile screens, and for clinical treatment names. The workhorse contrast to the display serif.
- **The bold/regular mix** in H1s (e.g. **Doctor-led** aesthetic medicine, *elevating beauty since 2010*) uses Bodoni's full weight axis — one colour, weight contrast only, per Blacklyne DNA.

**Why NOT the previously-used faces:** Fraunces (PECS / Passionate Lives), Editorial New (PhysioLogix / 21 Degrees / MK), PP Neue Montreal (Electricians4London / JMB), GT Sectra (HomeCert), Migra (Angel Plumbing), Libre Caslon (Angel Plumbing). Bodoni Moda has not been used and carries the most overtly *beauty-editorial* DNA of the available serifs — the right cultural fit for premium aesthetic medicine.

## HERO CONCEPT

- **Composition:** Full-bleed aubergine canvas with a soft rose-champagne mesh glow and fine grain. Left column: eyebrow (`CENTURIA MEDICAL MAKATI · DOCTOR-LED · SINCE 2010`), a large Bodoni H1 with bold/regular mix — *"**Doctor-led** aesthetic medicine, priced in **plain sight.**"* — a factual subline naming the three doctors and two branches, and two CTAs side by side (**Start the Treatment Quiz** / **Message us on Messenger**). Right column: a floating "Free aesthetic-doctor consultation" card plus a live trust cluster (4.3★ · 115 Google reviews · 11,662 community). No cheesy stock face — an editorial typographic + glass composition that loads instantly and reads premium in 3 seconds.
- **One-of-a-kind hook:** the **Treatment Discovery Quiz** entry sits *in* the hero, not buried below. The single most price-anxious patient in Manila can begin getting a transparent, personalised plan within one click.
- **Why it serves the Filipino aesthetic patient psyche:** she has already compared 3–5 clinics and is anxious about hidden prices. The hero answers her two unspoken questions immediately — *"are these real doctors?"* (named, Centuria Medical) and *"will they hide the price from me?"* (no — start the quiz). Warmth + transparency + doctor credibility in the first viewport.

6. **Eight treatment divisions** — bento grid, each a doorway into the SEO treatment tree.
7. **Korean influence** — educational teaser; the methodology that justifies the premium.

9. **Three doctors** — faces + specialisms + per-doctor consult CTA. Tertiary credibility, and Doc Jam's patients see her elevated.
10. **Transparent pricing teaser** — honest "what does it cost?" framing with indicative ranges → full pricing page.
11. **Testimonials** — verified aggregate (4.3★/115/11,662) front and centre; verbatim quotes flagged for client supply (not fabricated).
12. **Both branches** — 9F + 17F, maps, branch contacts. Unified brand.

## WOW FACTOR — TREATMENT DISCOVERY QUIZ

- **What:** a 5-step island (concern → age → budget band → timeline → doctor preference) that returns a *personalised plan* — matched treatments from the live treatment database, indicative ₱ ranges (clearly labelled, never invented exact prices), and a free-consultation CTA via Messenger / Call / Form. Progress persists in `localStorage`; the result is shareable and the plan can be saved.
- **Why this business:** the Filipino aesthetic patient researches 3–5 clinics and fears hidden prices. The quiz captures her *while* she's comparing, educates her, pre-qualifies budget, frames cost transparently, and converts to a free consultation — turning the praised differentiator (transparency) into a working conversion engine.
- **Technical:** Astro island, vanilla TS, no framework. Concern→treatment mapping reads the same `treatments.ts` that powers the SEO pages, so the recommendations are always in sync with the catalogue. Keyboard-accessible, reduced-motion aware, no console errors.
- **Secondary wow — Before/After gallery:** filterable by division, honestly framed ("individual results vary, all photos with patient consent"), per-item book CTA. Image slots ready for real client photography.
- **Tertiary — Doctor profiles:** Dr. Tan, Dr. Tee, Doc Jam. Verified facts only; where licence numbers/board fellowships are unconfirmed, the page leads with specialisms + patient sentiment rather than fabricated credentials.

## COPY VOICE

- **Tonality:** editorial Asian aesthetic medicine — calm, confident, transparent, doctor-led, K-beauty fluent without being trendy. The voice of a 15-year clinic that earned trust by being straight with people.
- **Three example phrases in voice:**
  - *"You should know the price before you're on the table. We think that's the bare minimum."*
  - *"Korean technique isn't a buzzword here — it's a method: lighter doses, more layers, results that look like you on a good day."*
  - *"Fifteen years in the same building. The doctors you meet are the doctors who treat you."*
- **What this voice would NOT say:**
  - "Get glowing skin in 5 minutes!" (Western trendy)
  - "Beauty starts here!" (vague Belo-style)
  - "Affordable beauty solutions" (cheap-feeling)
  - "Best aesthetic clinic in Makati!" (unverifiable comparative claim)
  - Anything K-pop-influencer in register.

## ASSET HARVEST PROOF / HONESTY NOTES

- **Logo:** no usable existing web logo. Designed a clean Bodoni Moda wordmark **DERMAMEDICS** with rose-champagne mark on the "D" and `ELEVATING BEAUTY SINCE 2010` in Inter, wide-tracked caps beneath. Delivered as inline SVG component.
- **Real reviews — NOT fabricated.** Google/Facebook/Booky render reviews via JS / auth and could not be retrieved verbatim by research tools. We therefore anchor social proof on the **verified aggregates only** (4.3★ from 115 Google reviews; 11,662 Facebook community) and ship the testimonial cards as clearly-flagged placeholders for the client to replace with real, consented quotes. No named quote is invented.
- **Prices — NOT fabricated.** Dermamedics' own promotional prices could not be harvested. The site shows **indicative market ranges** (clearly labelled "indicative — confirmed at your free consultation"), never invented "from ₱X" exact prices. Real price list is a client-supply item.
- **"Doc Jam"** — full legal name + PRC credentials unverified; profile leads with role + specialism + patient sentiment, no fabricated licence number.
- **Editorial imagery:** built primarily with CSS/SVG art, brand-palette mesh gradients and grain (a deliberate anti-cheesy-stock choice that also guarantees no broken/hot-linked images). Photography slots are ready and flagged for real client/treatment photography.
- **Centuria Medical Makati** building context surfaced in hero trust strip, branches page, about, and footer.

## ⚑ BRAND CORRECTION (post-launch, client artwork received)

The locked aubergine/champagne palette in this document was a *positioning* hypothesis (elevate above the "pink salon" norm). The client then supplied their **real brand asset** - a Mega Sale promo showing the actual identity:

- **Real palette: hot magenta `#E10A82` + gold `#D9A53A` + warm cream `#FCF4F4`** (deep magenta `#8E0456` / plum `#4A0329` for dark sections). This now drives the whole site.
- **Real logo: "DermaMedics" gold wordmark + "Skin & Cosmetic Surgery Clinic"** (note camelCase + the clinic does cosmetic *surgery*, not just skin). Legal name updated accordingly.
- **Hero now features a K-beauty model photo** (slot at `/photos/hero-model.png`) with a **₱99 "Mega Sale" gold badge** echoing the client's real campaign.

Per the standing rule *"use the client's real brand colours + logo, never invent a palette"* ([[feedback-use-client-brand]]), the real brand overrides the hypothesised one. The execution keeps the 2027-editorial discipline (Bodoni display, gold-on-magenta hierarchy, generous whitespace, glass/grain) so it reads as *premium* magenta+gold, not cheesy salon. Everything else in this concept (archetype, transparent-pricing spine, quiz, honesty constraints) stands.
